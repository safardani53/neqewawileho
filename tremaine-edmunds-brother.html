Tremaine Edmunds Brothers [2023 Update]

Publish date: 2024-05-07

An American football linebacker Tremaine Edmunds is a second-generation NFL player with his older brothers: Trey and Terrell, who are also NFL stars. Their father, Ferrell, played six NFL seasons until retirement in 1994.

The two-time Pro Bowl plays for Buffalo Bills since 2018- the 2022-23 season being his 5th NFL season.

Currently, Tremaine’s brother Trey is a free agent- the Steelers released him ahead of the 2022 season. As for his other brother, Terrell is a safety for Pittsburgh Steelers & wears jersey number #34.

The three NFL packs grew up watching their father play American football and clung to it. Even more, they would help each other train to hone their skills on the field.

Sports gene in the family!

The Edmund brothers are all alums of Virginia Tech but sought careers with different teams after the draft.

Tremaine along with his big brother Terrell is the first set of brothers who got picked in the first round of the same NFL Draft (2018). The eldest one Trey went undrafted & signed with New Orleans Saints in 2017.

Tremaine Edmunds Brother (Trey Edmunds) | Age, Early Life & Education

The oldest of three Edmunds brothers, Trey Edmunds was born on December 30, 1994, in Danville, Virginia, to Ferrell Edmunds,  and Felicia Edmunds.

His full name is Ferrell Edmunds III. Trey, 6 feet 2 inches (1.88 m), is 28 years old.

With his younger siblings: Terrell & Tremaine, he attended Dan River High School and played football as a running back & linebacker.

As reported, his parents married in July 1994 after meeting in Miami, Florida. Ferrell then played for the NFL team Miami Dolphins while Felicia taught at an elementary school.

Over three years at high school, he recorded 2,596 rushing yards and 33 touchdowns in the offensive position, while the defensive stat records 143 tackles, two sacks, four interceptions & two forced fumbles.

Collegiate Career

A four-star recruit Trey Edmunds enrolled at Virginia Tech in 2013, and played there for three seasons until 2015.

The freshman season proved to be his college career-best with 675 rushing yards for 10 touchdowns & two receiving touchdowns on 155 yards from 12 games.

During his senior year in 2016, Edmunds transferred to the University of Maryland & completed 158 rushing yards for one touchdown in 5 games.

Trey Edmunds Went Undrafted In NFL

Edmunds signed with the New Orleans Saints signed as an undrafted free agent on May 15, 2017. 

As a running back for the Saints, he played for a year (in 16 games but started in one) & finished with one rushing touchdown.

On September 3, 2018, the Saints released Edmunds.

Two days later, on September 5, 2018, he was signed by the Pittsburgh Steelers, where his brother Terrell was a part of the team.

During his reign with the team, Trey had many impressive scores and records under his name. He played for the Steelers for nearly four years.

On July 26, 2022, Trey Edmunds was waived by the Steelers, ahead of the 2022 season. Currently, Trey is a free agent with no news of signing approaches.

Another Brother Of Tremaine Edmunds | Everything About Terrell Edmunds!

Born on January 20, 1997, in  Danville, Virginia, Terrell is the middle brother among the Edmunds brothers.

Like his brothers, he was inclined towards football from a very young age. At Dan River High School, Terrell became a football prospect in varsity teams for three seasons.

His high school career stats recorded 2,438 rushing yards and 33 touchdowns on 263 carries. Also, FeDerius Terrell Edmunds caught 43 passes for 396 yards and 3 receiving touchdowns.

In 2015, he enrolled at Virginia Tech for his collegiate career as a football safety. As a freshman, Terrell Edmunds played 11 games to earn 34 tackles (16 solo & 18 assists).

His sophomore and the junior season finished with 101 tackles (14 games) and 61 tackles (10 games) before declaring for the NFL draft.

Terrell Edmunds | NFL Draft & Professional Career

After forgoing his senior year, Terrell became the first-round 28th pick of the Pittsburgh Steelers in the 2018 NFL Draft.

Other notable picks of the Steelers in the draft were James Washington, Mason Rudolph, Chukwuma Okorafor, and Marcus Allen.

On July 24, 2018, Edmunds signed a four-year contract worth $10.80 million with the Steelers.

Terrell made his professional debut with the Steelers at the Cleveland Browns in 2018, joining the rosters: James Conner, Antonio Brown, Joshua Dobbs, and Ben Roethlisberger. 

In 2018, Edmunds scored many goals for his team, received a grade of 63.7 from Pro Football Focus, and ranked 66th among all safeties in 2018.

In 2019, he started in all 16 games to record 105 tackles. Further two seasons with the Steelers earned him 68 tackles & 89 tackles in the 2020 & 2021season respectively.

Edmunds’s contract with the Steelers expired in 2021, but the team decided to extend his year contract with him on April 25, 2022.
