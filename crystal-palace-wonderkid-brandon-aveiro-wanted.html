Crystal Palace wonderkid Brandon Aveiro, 17, a transfer target for Benfica after Portugal U18s call-

Publish date: 2024-10-09

CRYSTAL PALACE youngster Brandon Aveiro is being watched by Benfica after his call-up to Portugal Under-18s.

The 17-year-old striker has been at a training camp this month and has sparked interest in luring him from the Premier League for regular football.

Primeira Liga clubs were allowed to watch the sessions and Aveiro has impressed Benfica and Braga.

Bundesliga scouts have also tracked the teenager, who signed his first professional deal at Selhurst Park at the start of the season.

He has played for Palace’s Under-23s this season after starting at the club as an eight-year-old.

The youngster was promoted after stunning form in the Under-18s this season saw him score 14 goals and assist 10 times.

Ther was some other big news to come out of Selhurst Park this week.

Sun Sport reported how goalkeeper Wayne Hennessey has escaped punishment after he was accused of performing a Nazi salute at a German team-mates birthday party.

A photo was uploaded on fellow Palace star May Meyer's Instagram story in January that showed Hennessey out with team-mates on a night out.

It showed the 32-year-old Welshman with one hand near to his mouth and the other raised in the air.

Hennessey denied any misbehaviour, claiming he had “waved and shouted at the person taking the picture to get on with it" and "put my hand over my mouth to make the sound carry".

He insisted he had not been “making a completely inappropriate type of salute” in the picture “frozen in a moment by the camera” and was backed by Palace boss Roy Hodgson.

But FA disciplinary chiefs moved to charge the goalkeeper and open up an investigation.

However Hennessey, who has 82 caps for Wales, has been cleared after the charge was "not proven".
