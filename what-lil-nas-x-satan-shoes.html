What are Lil Nas X's Satan shoes?

Publish date: 2024-07-30

RAPPER Lil Nas X has faced backlash over a controversial pair of "Satan Shoes" he released in March 2021.

Lil Nas X was set to appear at court on July 19, 2021, to attend a hearing regarding the lawsuit which turned out to be a trailer for a new music video .

What are Lil Nas X's Satan shoes?

The rapper released a pair of black and red sneakers which are part of a collaboration between Lil Nas X and New York-based art collective MSCHF.

They come complete with a bronze pentagram and an inverted cross — plus a drop of real human blood.

As part of the limited-edition "drop" of 666 pairs, each sneaker’s air bubble sole contains red ink and "one drop" of human blood, according to MSCHF.

They cost $1,018 a pair and the price tag is a reference to the Bible passage Luke 10:18 that reads: "I saw Satan fall like lightning from heaven."

On March 29, 2021, the rapper revealed that all 666 pairs were sold in under a minute.

Why have Lil Nas X's satan shoes caused controversy?

At the time of Lil Nas X's controversial sneakers release, Nike was granted a temporary restraining order against MSCHF, the Brooklyn-based creative agency tasked with creating the rapper's sneakers.

The restraining order prohibited MSCHF from fulfilling any orders of the Satan Shoes.

Nike also reportedly sued MSCHF for trademark infringement.

When the suit came out, Nike accused MSCHF of tricking consumers into thinking they approved the devilish sneakers.

Nike stated that it "is in no way connected with this project" and accused Nas' collaborator company of buying the shoes from them, modifying them, and upping the price.

The company alleges that their famous Swoosh logo is still prominently featured on X's shoes.

Nike is suing for damages and wants a judge to block Lil Nas's notorious "Satan Shoe" sales.

Is Lil Nas X going to jail?

The rapper had initially claimed he had a court date for July 19, 2021.

He then posted a series of joke videos on TikTok mocking his ''court appearance.''

Lil Nas X shared a video with a caption: “When you have court on Monday over the Satan Shoes and might go to jail but your label tells you to keep making TikToks.”

In an Instagram Live video, the rapper told his fans that he would be appearing in court at 8am on Monday, Los Angeles time.

He wrote: "all jokes aside i can’t believe i might be going to jail. who’s going to make mildly funny tweets about being gay while i’m away."

The rapper uploaded a two-minute long video to social media showing a title slate that read: "NIKE v. LIL NAS X. SUPREME COURT. JULY 19, 2021."

The footage turned out to be a trailer for a new music video for his new Kanye West-produced single, “Industry Baby.”
