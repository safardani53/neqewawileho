Who Is Catriona Carey, DJ Carey Sister? Wikipedia And Age

Publish date: 2024-08-06

Former hurler DJ Carey was born and brought up with his siblings, including his well-known younger sister Catriona, in a large household in Kilkenny, Ireland.

Born to a family of athletes, DJ started practicing hurling from a young age. As a result, Carey was already a renowned hurler in his hometown before he was in college.

Besides him, two of his siblings later became professional athletes.

Likewise, his aunt, Peggy, won four All-Ireland camogie trophies, and his granduncle, Paddy Phelan, was one of the most popular hurlers in his country in the 1930s.

Born Denis Joseph Carey, DJ Carey is regarded as one of Ireland’s best hurlers ever. He started his club career with the Young Irelands in 1988 at 17 and stayed with the club for 25 years, leading them to several championship wins until 2013.

Carey made it to Kilkenny’s senior county team a year after joining Young Irelands and appeared in 138 National League and Championship matches until his retirement in 2005.

In his seventeen-year-long career, he won five All-Ireland Championships for Kilkenny. Besides, he won ten Leinster Championship medals and four National Hurling League medals.

Carey’s career achievements have undoubtedly placed him among the Irish hurling legends. Unfortunately, his frequent involvement in controversies over the years has somewhat tarnished his image.

Who Is Catriona Carey, DJ Carey Sister?

Wikipedia And Age

Catriona Carey is one of the six siblings of a famous Irish hurler, DJ Carey.

She was born to her parents, John and Maura Carey, in the late seventies in Kilkenny County, Ireland. Although her birth year is unavailable, she is currently in her mid-forties.

Raised in a sports-loving family, Catriona inherited athletic genes from her elders and began playing camogie and hockey early.

Her aunt, Peggy, was influential in raising her interest in camogie, which she openly acknowledges.

Carey won the Felie na nGael Camogie Division 1 competition for the Clara GAA club in 1992. She also played the game for the Kilkenny camogie team for many years.

However, she gained widespread fame for her international field hockey career, which spanned around eight years.

Catriona debuted for the Irish women’s national field hockey team in 1998. She played 72 international games, appearing in the 2005 Women’s EuroHockey Nations Championship and the 2006 Women’s Intercontinental Cup before retiring in 2006.

She continued playing club hockey for the Hermes Ladies Hockey Club for a few more years.

Criminal Charges Against Catriona Carey

The Irish court fined Catriona around $1585 after pleading guilty to knowingly producing a false invoice to the Revenue Commissioners linked with VAT in 2008. She worked as an accountant in her brother’s company during this time.

Many years later, in 2020, the authorities charged her again with fraud and theft after forging a cheque. 

Moreover, in recent years, she has been arrested several times for fraudulent business practices, including her company’s involvement in “providing financial services” without authorization, which is illegal in Ireland.

Aside from her frequent arrests for her involvement in financial scams, Catriona was arrested for driving without a license in 2022, for which she received a four-year driving ban.

DJ Carey Sister | Brother Martin Is Also A Former Hurler

DJ and Catriona Carey’s sibling, Martin, is a former hurler. Martin Carey learned the game, practicing in Kilkenny with his older brother and friends.

He was part of Kilkenny County’s senior team for around four years but primarily served as their substitute goalkeeper. 

During his brief stint with Kilkenny, he won two Leinster Senior championships, two All-Ireland Senior Championships, and an All-Ireland winner’s medal. 

Besides, he also played for the Young Irelands, winning two Kilkenny senior club championship medals.
