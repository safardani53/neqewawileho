Early Life And Family

Michael Redd was born on August 24, 1979, in Columbus, Ohio, to his father, James Redd, and his mother, Haji Redd. He grew up with a sister named Michelle Redd. 

He attended West High School and later went to the Ohio State University. Michael has been passionate about basketball since his high school days. 

Redd served as the offensive attacker for three years at Ohio State University, with average points of 21.9, 19.5, and 17.5, respectively.

As a sophomore, he and Scoonie Penn led Ohio State University to the NCAA Final Four. 

After his junior year, the athlete was selected by the Milwaukee Bucks in the second round (43rd overall) of the 2000 NBA Draft.

NBA Career

Milwaukee Bucks

Michael could not make an impact in his first season due to his position on the depth chart behind NBA All-Star guard Ray Allen.

However, it became clear that he was better than expected when he performed admirably in practice against Glenn Robinson, Ray Allen, and other colleagues.

After this, George Karl, the former Bucks head coach, rewarded Michael with more playing time. 

During Michael’s second NBA season, he averaged 11.4 points per game and shot 44.4 percent from the three-point range. 

On February 20, 2002, Redd set a then-NBA record by making eight three-point field goals in the fourth quarter while playing against the Houston Rockets.

Redd signed a $12 million, four-year offer sheet with the Dallas Mavericks in October 2002, but the Bucks matched it, and he remained in Milwaukee.

During the 2003–04 season, Michael’s effort was rewarded with full starting responsibilities.

Eventually, he began earning averages of 21.7 points per game en route to his first and only All-Star Game participation. 

After the 2004–05 season, Michael signed a six-year, $91 million contract with the Bucks.

Likewise, the player opted to stay with the Bucks over joining his hometown team, the Cleveland Cavaliers, and playing with LeBron James for less money.

Regardless, he enjoyed the most incredible season of his career in the 2006–07 season and established a Bucks record for most points in a game, averaging 26.7 points per game. 

Injury

On January 25, 2009, Michael ruptured his ACL and MCL in his left knee. As a result, he missed the remainder of the season.

Redd returned to the Bucks for the start of the 2009–10 season. However, on January 10, 2010, while playing against the Los Angeles Lakers, he tore his ACL and MCL in the same knee he had torn over a year before.

The next day, it was reported that he would be out for the remainder of the season. 

After 14 months off with the injury, Michael returned to the Bucks on March 28, 2011; he collected four assists on his homecoming.

The player scored his first basket since returning from injury on March 30, 2011, against the Toronto Raptors.

In the 2010–11 season, Michael appeared in ten games. The 6’6″ player had averaged 20 points per game in his 11 seasons with Milwaukee until he left.

Phoenix Suns

On December 29, 2011, the Phoenix Suns signed Michael to a one-year deal. Likewise, on January 12, 2012, Michael made his Phoenix debut, scoring 14 points in a defeat against the Cavaliers. 

On February 7, 2012, Redd returned to Milwaukee, scoring 14 points in a victory over the Bucks. The audience gave him a standing ovation.

In a victory over the Houston Rockets on March 18, 2012, he set a season-best 25 points.

Retirement

Michael announced his retirement from the NBA on November 6, 2013. After retirement, Redd established himself as a successful entrepreneur and venture capitalist.

He also hosts the famous “Betting On Yourself” podcast.

National Team Career

Michael Redd was a Team USA junior team member and competed in the 2007 FIBA Americas tournament, serving as a qualifier for the 2008 Summer Olympics.

Throughout the competition, he averaged nearly 15 points each game. Additionally, Michael established the qualification record for most 3-pointers made in a single game during the team’s game against Puerto Rico.

He also broke Penny Hardaway’s record of 22 three-pointers in the tournament, setting a new mark of 28.

In addition, Michael was a member of the 2008 Olympic team who went undefeated to earn back the gold medal.
