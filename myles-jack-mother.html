Myles Jack Mother La Sonjia Jack | Family & Education

La Sonjia Jack was born in Illinois, Chicago, to a family background with African-American roots. Though, she has remained mum about the detail of her early life and parents.

On her Instagram, La Sonjia, the momma of NFL player Myles Jack, often shares pictures of her family members- including her brother Jovan and mother (Myle’s grandma).

She doesn’t dwell on her past but focuses on a fruitful present and future. 

Mrs. Jack is an educated, cultured, knowledgeable, and accomplished woman.

After attending Evanston Township High School (1983-87), she enrolled at
Arizona State University in 1993 and graduated with a General Business degree in 1995.

Then, La Sonjia pursued her Master’s degree in Organizational Management from the University of Phoenix- graduated in 2003.

The thirst for learning for La Sonjia was yet to quench. So, she enrolled at the University of Virginia Darden School of Business- there, she studied Executive Leadership Development program from 2018 to 2019.

The Atlanta Business Journal has listed her as one of the Most Powerful Women In Business.

La Sonjia Jack (Myles Jack’s Mother) | Career Journey

While acting as a ladder to her son’s way to one of the top NFL linebackers, La Sonjia also carved her own path.

Her LinkedIn profile shows she currently works as AVP Retail Operations/Quality Assurance for Cox Communications Solutions. La Sonjia joined the company in February 2015.

Before this, the NFL star’s mother served at Microsoft in Seattle, Washington- she was Sr. Director of Business Programs from August 2013 to February 2015, after promotion from Sr. Director of Retail Stores (2011-13).

But all these success stories came after decade-long years of dedication.

In the late 90s, La Sonjia Jack worked as a Pacbell (SBC Communications) sales manager. Moreover, she gave her 11 years to AT&T (formerly Cingular Wireless)- serving in different positions:

  • National Distribution Director (1997- 2008)
  • Channel Development Manager (2004- 2005)
  • Channel Manager (2001-2004)

Moreover, she worked for T Mobile USA for 2 years; as Director Of Business of Sales Operations (2009-11) before promotion to Regional Director.

The UCLA Bruins Alliance Board member is also involved in charity work.

She launched a non-profit named The LJ Way Foundation, which aims to provide guidance & support to families to deal with complexities while nurturing young athletes.

Brief About Myles Jack

Born Myles David Jack on September 3, 1995, the Scottsdale, Arizona native is a linebacker for NFL’s Pittsburgh Steelers (2022- Now) with jersey number #51.

From Bellevue High School to the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA), the 6 feet 1-inch (1.85 m) tall player made impressive career stats.

Myles Jack became Pac-12 Defensive Freshman of the Year, and Pac-12 Offensive Freshman of the Year, in 2013.

After sustaining a knee injury that ended his 2015 season, Jack dropped out of UCLA and announced his intention to enter the draft.

The 2016 NFL Draft was the second-round pick of the Jacksonville Jaguars; the top 5 NFL picks were Jared Goff, Carson Wentz, Joey Bosa, Ezekiel Elliott, and Jalen Ramsey.

He played with the Jaguars for six seasons until the 2021-22 season.

While at Jaguars, he started in 82 out of 86 games- returning 46 yards interceptions, 97 fumble yards, and 335 solo tackles.

Ahead of the 2022-23 season, Jack agreed to a two-year deal with the Pittsburgh Steelers on March 17, 2022.

It would keep him an unrestricted free agent through the 2023-24 season.
