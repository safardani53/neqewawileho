2nd Shortest NBA Player [2023 Update]

Publish date: 2024-08-06

Earl Boykins is a former American basketball player and is popular for his short height in the NBA. Since he retired from organized basketball, Boykins has served as a coach for a couple of high school basketball teams.

In 2014, he was hired to coach the Douglas County High School varsity basketball team.

As of 2022, Earl Boykins is employed as an assistant coach with the UTEP Miners, as he was hired in this position in 2021.

Every sport has some requirements, and it seems like it is the height for basketball. Most NBA players are above 1.8 meters tall.

But, some players have proved that height does not matter as long as you have skill and talent.

Earl Boykins, a 1.65 meters tall basketball player, is the second shortest NBA player in history. He is also the lightest player, weighing 133 lb.

When Earl was playing for a high school team, he was mistaken for a ball boy due to his short stature. His height was a massive barrier for him to achieve his NBA dream.

That is the probable reason he got undrafted in the 1998 NBA Drafts. However, nothing comes your way if you have passion and skill.

Soon, Boykins became a popular NBA player playing for NBA teams as well as international league teams, becoming one of the highest-paid players in the Italian League.

This article will take you on a beautiful journey of Earl Boykins, on how he went on from being an undrafted NBA player to becoming one of the highest-paid players in the Italian league.

Furthermore, the article will also uncover more details on his height, personal life, and retirement. But, before digging into the details, let’s quickly look at his quick facts.

Early Life

Earl Boykins was born Earl Antoine Boykins in Cleveland, Ohio, on June 2, 1976. 

Talking about his family, he is the son of Willie Williams (father) and Charlene Horton (mother). Earl also has a sister named Charnella Boykins.

Boykins’s father was a cop who was a fixture in Cleveland’s recreational leagues. When Earl was three years old, his father would sneak him to the gym stashed in his bag.

His father would give him a tennis ball because his hands were too small, and he would dribble the tennis ball to sleep.

Boykins spent most of his childhood with his father and with other men he played in the gym. He learned to play with bigger players twice his height in the gym.

It was a crucial skill he needed to learn as a basketball player. He is thankful to his father, who helped him work on his weakness and make it his strength.

For education, Earl attended Cleveland Central Catholic High School and graduated from Eastern Michigan University.

Professional Career

Early Career

Earl Boykins was a famous player at Cleveland Central Catholic High School.

The Plain Dealer named Earl the finest Cleveland-area high school basketball player of the 1990s in 2015.

Boykins attended Eastern Michigan University on an athletic scholarship and played for the college basketball team.

Earl had a valuable role in leading Eastern Michigan to win the MAC Tournament. As a result, he was named to the Mid-American Conference first team.

Boykins scored second in the NCAA Men’s Division I Basketball Championship during his senior year. He owns a career record for total assists of 624 at Eastern Michigan University.

On February 27, 2011, Eastern Michigan University honored Boykins by retiring his No. 11 jersey.

Undrafted To Play In Major NBA Teams

Earl Boykins was not chosen in the 1998 NBA Draft. However, he was signed by five different NBA clubs on short-term deals.

Since Boykins wasn’t drafted, he had a hard time getting a long-term contract from NBA teams, as most signed him as a temporary player or as a replacement.

Boykins finally signed a five-year, $13.7 million deal with the Denver Nuggets in the 2003–2004 season.

On November 11, 2004, Boykins became the NBA’s shortest player to score 30 or more points when he netted 32 points in the Nuggets’ winning game.

However, Boykins was later traded to the Milwaukee Bucks in January 2007 after playing three full seasons with Denver.

Later, Boykins opted out of his contract after ending the season in Milwaukee and signed with the Charlotte Bobcats midway through the 2007–2008 season.

Becoming Highest Paid Player In Italian League

In the 2007-08 NBA season, Boykins chose to play in Europe and signed a one-year, $3.5 million worth of contract with Virtus Bologna of the Italian A-League.

Boykins was the highest-paid basketball player in the Italian League and included money from Bologna’s sponsorship and marketing arms.

But, Boykins had a feud with the team owner after he went to the US to visit his sick son without permission. This was later solved, and Boykins stayed with the team.

On April 26, 2009, Boykins and his team won the EuroChallenge Cup. He was later released in June 2009.

Returning To The NBA

After being released by the team, Boykins came back to NBA and signed with Washington Wizards in November 2009.

Boykins was a precious acquisition of the Wizard. He had a huge role in the team to win against the Milwaukee Bucks on December 2, 2009, as he scored two free throws.

His performance wowed his rival team so much that they signed him for one year contract on August 19, 2010.

Later, Boykins signed a 10-day deal with the Houston Rockets on March 26, 2012. After the contract was over, Boykins retired as an NBA player.

On December 12, 2022, Earl Thomas was mentioned on the website FOOTBALL as being the 7 Most Pro Bowl selections in UT history.

The Basketball Tournament

Boykins competed in The Basketball Tournament on ESPN for Pual Champions in the summer of 2017. He helped lead his team to two victories in the TBT Jamboree for the $2 million grand prize, securing Paul Champions’ spot as one of the 64 teams in the tournament.

Earl averaged 24.5 points, 4.0 assists, and 3.5 rebounds per game during the Jamboree.

He scored 25 points in their first-round match-up, assisting in a 78-74 win over the Talladega Knights. The team was led by former NBA players Josh Boone and Gray Forbes.

Boykins and the Champions eventually fell to the number one-seeded Untouchables in the second round.

Coaching Career

Usually, NBA players opt for a coaching career after retiring as an NBA player, and it was the same for Earl Boykins.

In 2014, Boykins was appointed as the head coach for Douglas County High School’s basketball team in Castle Rock, Colorado.

Currently, Boykins serves as an assistant coach for UTEP Miners. He was hired in 2021 and has been working there for a year.

Carroll senior big man Brendan Temple mentioned Earl Boykins after his winning match against Montana Western on December 2, 2022, interview.

He said, “He always had me working out in the summer with him and just learning every aspect of the game.”

“Learning how to use my body. Even though we had different body types- he’s 5-foot-5, and I’m 6-foot-7- he just knew what I was looking for and how to execute it.”
