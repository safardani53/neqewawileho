Brother Elias Ymer

The older brother of Mikael Ymer, Elias Ymer, born on 10 April 1996, is a highly talented Swedish tennis player who has made significant strides in his career.

He attained his career-high ATP singles ranking of World No. 105 on 11 June 2018.

In doubles, he achieved a career-high ranking of World No. 188 on 16 October 2017.

From 2017 to 2018, Ymer was coached by Robin Söderling, a former top-ranked Swedish player known for his achievements in the sport.

Söderling’s guidance likely played a crucial role in Ymer’s development and success during that period.

In the autumn of 2022, Ymer received a remarkable opportunity to further expand his horizons beyond tennis.

He was accepted as a student at Harvard Business School’s prestigious “Crossover into Business” program, joining a select group of ten other accomplished athletes.

This program allows Ymer to explore his interests and develop business acumen alongside his sporting career, showcasing his ambition and commitment to personal growth.

Mikael Ymer Suspended For 18 Months

Mikael Ymer, a 24-year-old Swedish tennis player ranked No. 51 in the world, has been suspended from professional tennis for 18 months following a successful appeal by the International Tennis Federation (ITF) to the Court of Arbitration for Sport (CAS).

Ymer had initially been cleared of three whereabouts failures, which are considered potential anti-doping rule violations.

Ymer stated that he had been charged with the violations in January 2022 for missing three out-of-competition test attempts within a 12-month period.

He had contested the charges in a hearing and was cleared by an independent tribunal in June 2022.

However, the ITF appealed the decision, leading to the CAS partially upholding the appeal and imposing an 18-month suspension.

Expressing his disagreement with the decision, Ymer took to Twitter, emphasizing that he believed he did not commit the third offense and found the decision to try him again and find him guilty unfair.

He also expressed surprise at the severity of the 18-month suspension, considering his innocence.

The CAS clarified that Ymer had accepted the first two whereabouts failures but contested the third, which was attributed to miscommunication with his agent.

The panel concluded that Ymer failed to ensure compliance with anti-doping regulations by neglecting to verify his whereabouts filing and assuming it would be corrected by his agent or tennis authorities.

Despite the suspension, Ymer recently achieved success at Wimbledon, reaching the tournament’s third round after defeating the ninth seed, Taylor Fritz.
