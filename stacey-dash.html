Stacey Dash Biography

Stacey Dash was born on January 20, 1966, as Stacey Lauretta Dash in the Bronx, New York City. Her ancestors are Bajan, African-American, and Mexican. Darien Stacey, Stacey’s brother, founded DME Interactive, the first publicly traded African-American-led Internet company. Damon Dash, Stacey’s first cousin, was the former CEO and co-founder of Roc-A-Fella Records. Dash graduated from Paramus High School in 1985.

Career

Stacey Dash debuted on television in the NBC crime drama Pilot Farrell, Valerie Harper, and Ed O’Neill (1982), but only lasted one episode. She was best known for her role as Michelle in The Cosby Show episode “Denise’s Friend” in 1985. She also appeared in The Fresh Prince of Bel-Air. Stacey’s first significant television role was in the 1988 series TV 101, which was canceled after 13 episodes.

Stacey’s breakthrough performance came in the Richard Pryor comedy Moving (1988). She also appeared in Mo’ Money and Renaissance Man at the same time. Stacey appeared as a femme fatale in both the low-budget film Illegal in Blue and the teen comedy Clueless in 1995. She played Cher’s high school best friend, Dionne Davenport, despite being 28 years old at the time. When Stacey played Dionne in the film, it became a television spin-off (1996-1999).

Stacey appeared in View from the Top (2003), Gang of Roses (2003), and Getting Played (2003) after the television series ended (2005). She also appeared in a few minor guest appearances on television shows. Eve and CSI: Crime Scene Investigation are two examples. She played Vanessa Weir in the failed television series The Strip, which was canceled after several episodes.

Carl Thomas’s music video for the single “Emotional” features Stacey (2001). Stacey appeared in Kanye West’s music video for the single “All Falls Down” (2004). She also appeared in Marques Houston’s video “Favorite Girl” (2006). Stacey created her own lingerie line, Letters of Marque (2006).

Stacey appeared in Nora’s Hair Salon II, I Could Never Be Your Woman, Fashion Victim, Ghost Image, and American Primitive, among other films (2007). She appeared in films such as Phantom Punch, Secrets of a Hollywood Nurse, and Close Quarters (2008). She appeared in the 2008 reality show Celebrity Circus. Stacey injured herself while training prior to the series premiere; however, she performed on the trapeze bungee throughout the show and was a finalist. She came in second place, trailing Antonio Sabàto, Jr.

Stacey was a recurring character on the television show The Game (2009). She played Valerie “Val” Stokes in VH1’s first scripted series, Single Ladies (2010). She left the show on August 31, 2011, to spend more time with her family. Stacey went on to play Lisa in Dysfunctional Friends and to act in Funny or Die (2012). Stacey Dash Is Normal used YouTube to broadcast trailers and shorts for her web series (2013).
