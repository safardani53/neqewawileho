Three men killed in crash as tributes paid to pals including brother who donated his kidney to save

Publish date: 2024-02-15

TRIBUTES have been paid to three men who were killed in a tragic crash.

The fatal accident happened on Monday, February 13, in Decoy Drive, Eastbourne.

The victims have been named as Daniel Davis, 21, Jonny Day, 32 and Jon 'Tommy' Miller, 36.

All three, who were from Berkshire, were sadly pronounced dead at the scene.

Their heartbroken families have today released statements remembering their loved ones.

Jon 'Tommy' Miller's family described him as a "hero" as his kidney was donated to his sister.

They said: “We as a family would like to pay tribute to our dear brother who has sadly left us. Our hearts are broken.

“Our Tommy was truly one of a kind - he was funny, smart and had the biggest heart.

"He was loved by so many and he impacted everyone who knew him.

“Tommy donated his kidney to his sister which saved her life. He will forever be our hero.

"We would like to be given the respect and space to grieve our devastating loss.”

A statement on behalf of Jonny's family read: “Jonny was the person who lit up any room. He was so loved by everyone that met him.

“A loving partner, father, son and brother, we will miss him endlessly. As his partner I have lost my whole life.”

Daniel's family said he was "always going to be missed".

They said: “Rest in peace angel, we love you so much – sleep tight. We all loved you so much, precious Dan Dan.”

His older brother added: “Fly high, little bro. I hope Heaven gives you the best bed up there until we meet again – your big bro, Smallz.”

Meanwhile Daniel's best friend said: “Since the day I met you 16 years ago, you have been my brother and my best friend.

"I love you with all my heart brother, and we’re always thinking of you.”

Emergency services were called to the scene at around 11.10pm after reports of a collision involving a silver Citroen Berlingo.

Cops say an investigation is ongoing and the families are being supported by specialist officers.

A grey Range Rover Sport believed to have been involved has been located and recovered.

Two men from Eastbourne, aged 38 and 40, were arrested on suspicion of murder.

They have since been released on bail.

Detective Superintendent Till Sanderson said: “This is a fast-moving, complex investigation into an incident in which three men sadly lost their lives.

“I would like to extend my condolences to their families and next of kin, who are being supported by specialist officers.

“Enquiries are ongoing to establish the exact circumstances around Monday’s events and there will continue to be an increased police presence in the area.

“Anybody who witnessed the collision, saw any suspicious driving in the area around that time or has relevant CCTV, doorbell or dashcam footage, is asked to contact police online or via 101, quoting Operation Brinmore.”
