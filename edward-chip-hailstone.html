How Did Edward “Chip” Hailstone Pursue His Career? Professional Life

Hailstone and his family’s lives were mostly out of the spotlight until their way of life piqued the interest of television show producers, who invited them to be a part of an upcoming documentary television series called “Life Below Zero.” The series, which airs on the National Geographic Channel but is produced by BBC Worldwide, aims to highlight the daily and seasonal activities of hunters who have made a living in remote areas of Alaska. The title of the show alludes to the fact that Alaska is mostly enveloped in subzero temperatures for the majority of the year. The family, as well as a number of other cast members, are shown in their daily struggles, depending on what they require to survive.
The family lives near the Kobuk River in Nordic, Alaska, which is 19 miles (30 kilometers) north of the Arctic Circle in a very cold region of the state. Hailstone’s wife is shown with family ties that go back thousands of years, so she has intimate knowledge of surviving in Alaska taught to her by her family through the generations. Agnes is one of the show’s main characters, but the entire family appears on a regular basis.

The show was a huge success from the start, and it was nominated for a Primetime Creative Arts Emmy Award for Outstanding Cinematography for a Reality Program in 2015. It won the award the following year, and in 2017 it won an Emmy Award for Outstanding Picture Editing for an Unstructured Reality Program.
