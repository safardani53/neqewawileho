A14 traffic delays after major crash in Cambridgeshire involving five vehicles blocks both carriagew

Publish date: 2024-08-12

FIVE people were injured after a major crash involving five vehicles blocked both sides of the A14 and led to long delays.

The horrific incident in Cambridgeshire happened at about 9am and one person had to be cut from the mangled wreckage of their car.

Police have described details of the crash as "sketchy at this time" and advised motorists to avoid the area.

A fire service spokeswoman said: "Three fire engines from Sawtry, Huntingdon and St Neots were called to a road traffic collision at 9am this morning, on the A14 Eastbound between the Spitalls roundabout at Huntingdon and Godmanchester.

"Five vehicles were involved and there were five casualties.

"Firefighters used specialist equipment to extricate one casualty who was left in the care of ambulance staff before returning to their stations just before 10am."

An ambulance spokesperson said it had treated a male and female patient with non-life threatening injuries.

All lanes were reopened shortly before 2pm.
