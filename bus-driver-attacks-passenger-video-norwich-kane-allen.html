Bus driver battered passenger in shocking street brawl after row over him pulling off too fast from

Publish date: 2024-07-17

A PASSENGER filmed brawling with a bus driver on a busy city street hit the roof because he pulled away too fast, a court heard today.

Stunned commuters looked on as furious Kane Allen, 20, traded blows with driver Paul Roberts, 44, on the path outside Stadia bar in Norwich in May.

The astonishing punch-up was filmed on mobile phone and shared widely on social media.

Roberts, of Wisbech, Cambs, got an 18-month conditional discharge after being found guilty of assault on Wednesday.

Allen, of Norwich, gave evidence in the trial against the disgraced driver at the city’s magistrates’ court.

He was back at court today to be sentenced himself after admitting threatening and abusive behaviour with intent to provoke a response.

Trouble blew up after Allen jumped on the popular X29 bus at Drayton Road, Norwich.

He was walking to his seat but felt the driver hared off too quickly, making him stumble, JPs heard.

Jane Foster, prosecuting, said that at the next stop raging Allen went to argue with the driver and shouted and swore at him.

Roberts stayed in his seat but Allen got “caught in the doors”, the court heard.

The bus moved off but stopped near Stadia bar.

Allen was still screaming and swearing as Roberts leapt out of his seat and pushed the fuming passenger off his packed vehicle.

Dramatic footage showed the driver, wearing a Stagecoach fleece, dragging Allen from the road onto the pavement by his jacket.

He then swung him round a couple of times causing the pair to fall.

As the pair continued to grapple with each other, a passer-by tried to intervene.

Allen then swung for the driver but missed as shocked passengers filed off the bus.

He then squared up to the X29 driver before getting smacked in the face.

Allen could then be heard yelling: "You f*****g c***".

Leaning out of the bus door, the driver responded: "Well you're big enough to be bringing it on."

The footage was posted on Twitter earlier by Aaron Clarke with the caption: "The bus drivers in #norwich are ghetto as f***!"

Ms Foster told JPs: “They were both scuffling on the pavement.”

People on the bus gave statements and were concerned by Allen’s threatening behaviour.

Ms Foster said: “They were concerned there would be a fight and that’s exactly what happened - there was a fight.”

Stephen Poole, defending, said Allen, who has disabilities and mental health problems, was injured in the set-to.

Mr Poole said he suffered bruising to his face after getting “a right thump around the side of his head”.

He said Allen had “real anger problems as a young person” but had since sought help and was much calmer.

Mr Poole said he had a partner with two children and admitted that he had to “grow up”.

Allen received an 18-month conditional discharge and was told to pay £85 costs and a £20 victim surcharge.

Andy Campbell, Managing Director of Stagecoach East, said: "We can confirm that a bus driver was suspended on Wednesday (3 May) following an incident in Norwich earlier that day.

"We understand on-street video footage of part of the incident has subsequently been posted on social media."

It comes after a hero bus driver booted a drunk off his bus in front of cheering passengers after he spat in his face in February.
