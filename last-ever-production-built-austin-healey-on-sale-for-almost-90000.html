Last ever production-built Austin Healey on sale for almost 90,000

Publish date: 2024-08-27

CONSIDERED one of Britain's most iconic sports cars of the 1950's and 60's, the Austin Healy is renowned for its performance and style.

The love-child of a merger between the Austin division of the British Motor Company, and the Donald Healey Motor Company, the most popular 'big' version of the sports car was produced up until 1967 when the factory shut down.

Relatively rare among traders, classic car nuts in the UK now have a chance to purchase a piece of sports car history.

This 1967 3000 MKIII model is the last of the 'big' Austin Healey vehicles to be produced - a smaller, and less popular, Sprite model was made until 1971.

Being auctioned off by Classic Car Auctions at the NEC in Birmingham on April 2, it is expected to fetch as much as £90,000.

Holding chassis number HBJ843025, the vehicle was produced on 21 December 1967, just a few months before the factory shut down.

It is the last Healey to be built in full by the workforce, while the production line was fully operational - an additional three were built from parts in early 1968, but were not authentic production models.

After being originally registered to the Donald Healey Motor Company, it has seen just two owners, remaining with its current owner since 1972.

Featuring fresh Golden Beige Metallic paint from a recent makeover, all facets of the 150bhp, 3.0-litre six-cylinder engine are said to be in "almost pristine" condition.

'This has got to be the most significant, 'road going' Big Healey on the market today and as such represents an amazing opportunity for classic car collectors," Richard Greenhalgh, classic car specialist at auction host Classic Car Auctions, said.

"The car has great shut-lines, a deep shine and lots of detail."

If the car does reach the estimated £90,000 price tag, it will be recorded as one of the most expensive Austin Healeys ever sold.
