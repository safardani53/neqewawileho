Jake Marisnick | Injuries and Controversies

Jake Marisnick had a minor collision with the opponent catcher Jonathan Lucroy of the Los Angeles Angels on July 7, 2019, while trying to score on a flyout.

The collision, despite being unintentional, was called a breach of the Official Baseball Rule 6.01.

The opponent, Jonathan, had a broken nose and a concussion that required surgery.

After the incident, Jake stated that he had made the last-minute decision to move toward the inside of the plate, which collided him with Jonathan.

“I was trying to clear all the way to the inside, and that’s why I’m so far inside, and the throw to the plate took him a little bit more inside than I anticipated. I just kind of explained and apologized. I’ll let him tell his side of the story, and I don’t want to put words in his mouth.”
-Jake Marisnick

Marisnick hit by a Fastball

On 18 July 2019, against the Los Angeles Angels, Jake was hit by a pitch by the Angels’ reliever Noe Ramirez.

To elaborate, Ramirez had first served two sliders followed by an 89.6-mph fastball, which hit Marisnick around his left shoulder.

“I was just trying to sneak a fastball by him. I think the scouting report on me is I might sneak a fastball in there after a couple of offspeed pitches.”
-Noe Ramirez

Therefore, as both the teams were jawing on each other, umpire Stu Scheurwater issued warnings to both clubs.

Following it, even after the match was benched, things were getting heated between the teams. However, Marisnick kept it peace, waiving his teammates to defuse the situation.

“I mean, there’s no place for anything like that on a baseball field. I’m doing my best just to kind of calm down the situation.”
-Jake Marisnick

All in all, the incident was handled as before with an undisclosed fine and suspension of games.
