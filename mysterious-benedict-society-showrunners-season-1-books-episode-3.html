Mysterious Benedict Society showrunners reveal how season 1 will follow the books' 'structure' ahead

Publish date: 2024-02-22

THE Mysterious Benedict Society showrunners revealed how closely Season 1 will follow the books, written by Trenton Lee Stewart, in a new interview about the Disney+ show.

The next episode of the young adult series, which premiered with two episodes on June 25, comes out on Friday, furthering the adventures of Tony Hale’s Mr. Benedict and the four children he’s recruited.

Originally set at Hulu, The Mysterious Benedict Society moved to Disney+ in November 2020 and finally had its two-episode premiere last week.

Episode 3 releases on Friday, and fans of the beloved book series can expect more of what they know and love from the novels.

The show follows as “four gifted orphans are recruited by the peculiar Mr. Benedict for a dangerous mission to save the world from a global crisis known as The Emergency,” according to Disney+.

Showrunners Matt Manfredi and Phil Hay opened up about adapting the books, of which there are four in the main series, as well as a companion book and prequel, in an interview with The Wrap.

The pair revealed Season 1 will stay very true to its source material, with Phil explaining simply: "The first book is this first season, basically."

He continued: "It really did lend itself to that kind of structure."

Meanwhile, Matt shared that one of the aspects from the books that felt especially important to adapt well is "this emphasis on empathy, this emphasis on the love and importance of truth."

He said it's been "pretty exciting to explore" this emphasis, as well as "the idea of these kids whose superpowers are things that perhaps they don’t see as valuable first."

Matt added: "They can’t fly, they can’t shoot lasers, but they have empathy. They’re resilient, they’re inventive.

"They think about things in unique ways. And those are the things that make them special, make them valuable — heroes."
