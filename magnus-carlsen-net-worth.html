Career

In his early childhood, Magnus was guided to play chess by his father. During his teens, he was mentored by the champions Ringdal Hansen and Simen Agdestein.

Carlsen won against some of the toughest seniors in the Junior Norwegian Championships in 2000. Later in 2003, he won the World Under-12 Championship. 

Four years later, he won the Norwegian Chess Championship. Carlsen became the world champion in 2013 and has maintained the title ever since.

In January 2015, Carlsen won the 77th Tata Steel Chess Tournament, which was played mainly in the Wijik ann Zee. 

He faced Sergey Karjakin in the 2016 World Chess Championship, which ended in a 6-6 draw. However, Magnus managed to win the game in a 3-1 victory and retain the World Champion title.

Carlsen competed in the 82nd Tata Steel Chess Tournament on January 10, 2020, where he finished two points behind the winner Caruana.
