Cheltenham Festival 2017 - Fox Norton set to head for the Champion Chase and miss the Ryanair

Publish date: 2024-04-26

FOX NORTON is set to take on Douvan in the Champion Chase at Cheltenham on Wednesday.

The seven-year-old made a sparkling debut for Colin Tizzard when landing the Shloer Chase at Prestbury Park in November, but was then sidelined by injury for three months.

He proved no match for Altior on his comeback run in the Game Spirit Chase at Newbury, but connections are confident he will raise his game at the Festival.

Fox Norton also holds an entry in the Ryanair Chase, but connections prefer a crack at the two-mile chasing crown.

Joe Tizzard, assistant to his father, said in his Coral blog: "I think Fox Norton will more than likely run in the Champion Chase. He schooled beautifully on Tuesday.

"He was a bit stiff after Newbury but he was entitled to be given the injury he'd picked up at Cheltenham the time before.

"He'll take his chance in the Champion Chase. If it's still good to soft ground that should be fine. I think he'll have a better chance the slower the ground, rather than top of the ground.

"He deserves to take his chance in this and is a worthy second-favourite.

"Take Douvan out and it's a very open race."
