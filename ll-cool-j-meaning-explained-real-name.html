What does LL Cool J stand for? Meaning explained

Publish date: 2024-04-15

LL Cool J is an American rapper, record producer and actor.

The two-time Grammy winner, who was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 2021 has also carved out a successful film career.

What does LL Cool J stand for?

LL Cool J stands for Ladies Love Cool James.

The moniker was coined by his friend and fellow rapper Mikey D.

He had originally called himself J-Ski but, as he told the VHS1 documentary Planet Rock: The Story of Hip Hop and the Crack Generation, he did not want to be associated with cocaine culture – with a few rappers at the time using the names Ski or Blow, which were connected to the use of the narcotic.

What is LL Cool J's real name?

LL Cool J was born James Todd Smith on January 14, 1968 in Bay Shore, New York.

His parents were Ondrea Griffith and James Louis Smith Jr, aka James Nunya.

According to a Chicago Tribune report, his father shot his mother and grandfather, nearly killing them.

A four-year-old LL Cool J is said to have found them with blood everywhere.

Inspired by the likes of The Treacherous Three, LL Cool J started rapping at the age of 10 and began making his own mix tapes when he was 16.

Signing to the Def Jam record label he released his first single, I Need a Beat, in 1984.

The 12” single went on to sell more than 100,000 copies and helped to establish both LL Cool J and Def Jam.

He was one of the early hip hop artists to gain mainstream success, along with the likes of Run DMC and Kurtis Blow.

Over the years he has released 13 albums.

VH1 placed him in their 100 Greatest Artists Of All Time" list in 2010.

LL Cool J has also appeared in a number of films including In Too Deep, Mindhunters and Edison.

He has also plays Special Agent Sam Hanna in NCIS: Los Angeles.

He married Simone Johnson in 1995 and they have four children.

Why did LL Cool J change his name?

Smith started using the moniker LL Cool J in 1984 when he was 16.

He’d tried out a number of stage names but he wanted something memorable.

"I had a friend, his name is Playboy Mikey D, he's a rap artist," he told Katie Couric in 2013. "We were in a group together. I was walking down the street one day, and I said, 'I want a cool name'."
