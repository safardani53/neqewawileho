Your Susan B Anthony coin may be worth $16,800 - the confusing detail that make so valuable

Publish date: 2024-08-23

YOU may want to check your spare change as a Susan B Anthony (SBA) dollar coin has sold for $16,800.

After 20 years of being out of production, the U.S. Mint decided to bring the coin back but made an error in the process.

As we’ve stated time and time again, errors are what make certain coins extremely rare and valuable.

This particular tip comes from a coin collector known as TreasureTown on TikTok.

He recently posted a video revealing why this SBA dollar coin sold for nearly $17,000.

The coin is worth that price due to what’s called a transitional wrong planchet error.

This means that the dollar coin has the planchet of a 2000 Sacagawea dollar, but the design on both the obverse and reverse is that of the 1999 SBA dollar coin.

In addition, the coin had a grade of MS66 from Professional Coin Grading Services - adding more value.

MORE ON THE SBA COIN

The SBA coins were produced on a few separate occasions: 1979 to 1981 and in 1999.

The coin replaced the Eisenhower Dollar and was actually the first time that a woman appeared on a U.S. circulating coin, according to the U.S. Mint.

It was designed by Frank Gasparro in efforts to honor women’s suffrage leader, Susan B. Anthony.

The obverse features the profile of Susan B. Anthony while the reverse shows an American eagle landing on the Moon.

The Mint says this is an adaptation of the Apollo 11 insignia used on the Eisenhower Dollar.

After 22 bids, one lucky coin collector scored the SBA coin for an impressive $16,800.

A GUIDE TO COIN VALUES

This particular dollar coin had such a high value due to its grade and error.

Errors happen when the mint incorrectly manufactures a coin, leading to elements that are not supposed to be included.

However, experts determine a coin's value based on a variety of factors.

This includes mintage, errors, demand, age, design, and condition.

More importantly, coins are graded on a rigid scale which is what collectors will pay big bucks for.

There are five main components when it comes to a grading scale including strike, surface preservation, luster, coloration and eye appeal.

Coins are assigned a whole number between 1 and 70, with the highest figure being flawless.
